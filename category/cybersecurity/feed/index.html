]]> Ever wonder how hackers can get into your computers? Your anti-virus is up to date, you have your firewall set correctly, what gives?

Did you know that 80% of hacking related breaches occur due to stolen or weak passwords. Some of these can be due to something as complex as cracking stolen hashes, to something as simple as weak passwords. Do you or your employees write down your password into a sticky note and place them on your monitor? Are you using passwords such as “Password” “abc123” or even “qwerty”? If so, STOP, you may as well not use one at all. This is equivalent to parking your car at the Cumberland Mall, putting your keys on the hood and walking inside. Sure it will keep honest people out.

So complex passwords are tough to remember, but you can make them simpler. Using different phrases such as “The big red elephant has 4 feet at the bottom and 2 ears on top!” can allow you to complicate your password without forgetting it. For instance using the phrase above could result in a password of “Tbreh4f@tb&2eot!” which would complicate the password, provide length, and different character types. If you don’t feel like doing that, using a password manager is another option.

And if you feel that you don’t need complex passwords, just keep using those simple passwords, just be sure you can afford downtime if your computers fall victim to cyber attacks.

Password managers, when used correctly, can be that solution for not only keeping your passwords secure, but also saving you time. Additional security features like 2FA (2 Factor Authentication) and other authentication methods (such as using a Yubi-Key) will provide one more layer of security.

And remember, your password manager is only as secure as the password you use to secure it. Don’t expect your password manager to be secure when you use “password123” to lock it.

The post How Secure is your Data? appeared first on Init Cyber Solutions.

]]> With the “price tag” on data ranging from minimal to “Business Critical” or “Irreplaceable” what do you value your data at?

Today, Data breaches, loss of information due to being hacked, or even worse, ransomware are on the continual rise. One “piece of mind” that you can have is utilizing data backups.

Trust me, from what I have seen, those who claim they are performing “Data Backups” really aren’t. The act of simply copying and pasting onto an external hard drive isn’t a good solution for data backup. Worse yet, some just have two copies of the data on the same hard drive and call it a backup.

What do you value your data at? Is your data business critical data or some family photos? Would you be able to get them back if your computer crashed? What if your building caught fire, floods or lightning strikes it?

An Off-site data solution is highly recommended in addition to any local backups you conduct (i.e. on an external hard drive). Whether you choose the “Cloud” to host your data, or performing backups once a month/week/day ensure you follow the 3-2-1 rule. You need three (3) Copies of data. Two (2) of them need to be on different storage medias other than where you primarily store it. Finally one (1) of these storage medias needs to be offsite. Following this rule at a minimum, coupled with differential, incremental, or full backups at regular intervals will help secure the availability of your data. Your data, whether it is business critical information or digital photos/memories are just as important to a company as it is to a consumer.

So once again, how secure is your data?
